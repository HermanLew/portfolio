# Portfolio Concept

Version: 1.0

---

# Core Direction

The website is a premium portfolio for Herman Lewandowsky, a Lead Product Designer.

The primary impression should be:

"This designer can turn product complexity into scalable systems."

The site should feel like a calm editorial product portfolio, not a landing page, agency site, or visual showcase. It should communicate product thinking, systems thinking, leadership, and execution quality.

---

# Positioning

Primary positioning:

Product thinker.

The portfolio should present Herman as someone who can own complex product problems from strategy to execution. The visual language should support credibility, clarity, and maturity.

The tone should be moderately personal: enough authorial voice to reveal thinking and judgment, but never overly biographical or self-promotional.

---

# Visual Character

The design should feel:

- Calm
- Confident
- Mature
- Editorial
- Premium
- Precise
- Structured

Avoid:

- Loud visual effects
- Startup-like energy
- Agency-style presentation
- Dribbble-style polish for its own sake
- Decorative gradients, blobs, glass, neon, or cursor effects

The site should be restrained but memorable. It should use one or two strong structural ideas rather than many visual tricks.

---

# Color And Surfaces

Use a dark editorial base with subtle surface changes.

Primary background:

- #0A0A0A

Secondary surfaces:

- #111111
- #151515

Use subtle changes in surface tone to create rhythm through the page. The palette should stay quiet and premium.

Avoid colorful accents by default. Use white, gray, spacing, and typography for emphasis.

---

# Typography

Typography is the main visual language.

Guidelines:

- Large, confident hero headline.
- Short paragraphs.
- Strong hierarchy.
- Comfortable reading width.
- No decorative or condensed fonts.
- Do not over-style section titles.

Text tone:

- Slightly explanatory but concise.
- Show expertise through precise language.
- Avoid long marketing copy.

---

# Motion

Motion should be calm but alive.

Allowed:

- Subtle fade
- Soft translateY
- Light stagger when it improves reading flow
- Quiet hover transitions
- Snap scroll in project section
- Scroll-driven horizontal motion in expertise section

Avoid:

- Bounce
- Elastic motion
- Large scale effects
- Rotation
- Parallax abuse
- Cursor effects
- Fancy loaders
- Animation chains

Motion should support orientation and hierarchy, not become the attraction.

---

# Responsive Direction

Desktop first, but mobile must be intentionally redesigned.

Desktop:

- Editorial grid.
- Fullscreen project snap sequence.
- Horizontal scroll-driven expertise.

Mobile:

- Hero stacks vertically.
- Portrait becomes restrained and secondary.
- Projects become vertical, readable sections.
- Expertise becomes vertical blocks.
- No horizontal scrolling.
- Preserve spacing, hierarchy, and editorial feeling.

---

# Implementation Notes

Preferred stack:

- Next.js
- React
- TypeScript
- Tailwind CSS
- Framer Motion

Implementation principles:

- Feature-based structure.
- Semantic HTML.
- Accessible focus states.
- Reduced motion support.
- Optimized images.
- No unnecessary dependencies.
- Keep components focused and readable.

The build should always reinforce the core impression:

"This designer understands products, leads teams, and builds scalable systems."
